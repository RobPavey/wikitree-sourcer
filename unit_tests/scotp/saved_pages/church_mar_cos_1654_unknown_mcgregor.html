Gender:

During indexing, sometimes the sex of an individual could not be clearly distinguished on the original document.

The forename or abbreviated forename might be ambiguous, for example, Marion could be male or female and the entry might simply note 'lawful child of' rather than 'son or daughter of.'

In such cases, 'U' for Unstated was entered in the sex column to ensure that the wrong sex was not attributed. 'U' values are included in 'Male', 'Female' and 'Both' searches.

Selecting 'Both' will cover any entries where the sex of the child was recorded incorrectly.

Enter the name of either the bride or groom to narrow the search.

The bride's surname is usually the maiden surname, but there may be occasions where the married surname was recorded instead.
